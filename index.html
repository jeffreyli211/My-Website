Hey there! I'm Jeffrey Li.


Who am I?

My name is Jeffrey Li. Born and raised in New York City, I moved to Boston in 2017 to pursue a Bachelor's degree in computer science at Boston University (class of 2021). I'm an aspiring software engineer and passionate about using my skills as a programmer for a meaningful cause. On the side, I enjoy spending my time rock climbing/bouldering, building computers, and playing a variety of different video game titles.

Why computer science?

Much of my passion for computer science sparked from my love of video games throughout my childhood. Growing up with two older brothers who constantly brought home the latest games to play from various different genres. From The Legend of Zelda, to Resident Evil, to Portal, I quickly grew fond of the virtual worlds that all these different titles brought me into. Upon entering my first year of college in 2017, I carried this fascination with me as I wondered about what made them tick. How do all the games that I fell in love with work?

This curiosity drove me to explore the field of computer science. In the process of learning the answers to my many questions about games and game development, I discovered a new passion in the form of programming. Every new programming project I took on felt like a new game being played, with a new type of puzzle to solve.

Projects

A collection of projects that I have worked on or am currently working on. You can find these projects on my github profile as well: My Github Page

Triangle Rasterization

This project was one of my first times being exposed to computer graphics and rendering methods, and demonstrates the fundamental technique of rasterizing lines and triangles on a digital canvas. Despite being a rather basic concept for shape-drawing, rasterization has proven to be very crucial within computer graphics, primarily in rendering 3D models of objects. Overall, its simplicity in concept paired with its significance in application made this project a great introduction into the world of computer graphics.

3D Spider Modeling

My next project focused on creating a 3D model creature, with the inclusion of movable joints. Diving further into the realm of computer graphics, this project demonstrates the foundations used to simulate animation in moving objects or animals. Given the many moving parts of a spider, I found it to be a perfect candidate to showcase these features. Although this project was more difficult than I originally anticipated, it was nonetheless a fun and challenging way to work in a 3D environment.

3D Vivarium Simulation

Using my experience gained from 3D modeling and joint manipulation, this project simulates the relationship between predator and prey fish within a 3D vivarium/aquarium. Each creature in the tank has been implemented with swimming animations, and swims in the direction best suited for its role. More specifically, predators swim in the direction that best increases its chances of catching a prey, whereas a prey will swim to best increase its chances of escaping the surrounding predators.

Scenes with Shading and Lighting

My latest computer graphics project showcases the different techniques that are involved in creating a 3D scene, including light sources and shading methods. With so many objectives to accomplish, this was one of the most elaborate and challenging graphics projects I have done. All shapes within the scene were created from scratch, using the same triangle rasterization approach as my first graphics project, and different forms of light sources were added per scene to demonstrate ambient, infinite, and point light sources.
